Sunmint Program

Restoring our amazon one tree at a time

SunMint empowers small Amazonian farmers to restore the rainforest through sustainable cacao farming using cabruca agroforestry. By providing critical resources and innovative financing, we promote biodiversity, combat climate change, and secure a sustainable supply of high-quality cacao while fostering economic opportunities for indigenous communities.

WHITEPAPER

Problem statement

problem statement

The Amazon rainforest faces ongoing degradation from cattle ranching and soybean farming, threatening biodiversity and contributing to climate change. Indigenous communities are often marginalized, lacking access to economic opportunities. While rising cacao prices make sustainable cacao farming a promising alternative for land restoration, farmers face significant barriers: high upfront costs and a 1.5-year yield gap before cacao trees bear fruit.

Our solution

our solution

SunMint's program supports Amazonian farmers by providing upfront financing for high-quality cacao trees, tools to track planting and management, and administrative support for issuing and selling carbon credits to US companies. Revenue from carbon credits sustains the program, enabling farmers to overcome financial barriers, restore the rainforest, and achieve economic integration. This holistic approach boosts biodiversity, mitigates climate change, and empowers indigenous communities with sustainable livelihoods.

How it works

Part 1

Tree Financing Finance

Supporters fund cacao tree planting through merchandise sales, empowering farmers to adopt agroforestry, restore the Amazon ecosystem, and earn fair returns. Merchants integrate our tree-planting feature into products, with QR codes linking to the QR Code Registry to track purchases and impact. Registered farms are tracked in the Farms Registry.

View farms participating in the Sunmint program. The Farms Registry tracks details like farm name, location, planting area, and ownership, ensuring transparency for carbon credit verification.
