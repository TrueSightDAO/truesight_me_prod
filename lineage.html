Lineage · Experiential-Learning Credentialing

Credential the people your program transforms

Lineage is TrueSight DAO's experiential-learning credentialing network. Schools, studios, dojos, cohorts, and communities issue portable, verifiable credentials for their members — co-stamped by the DAO, discoverable from a printed QR, and owned by the learner. Optionally, plant a cacao tree in honour of each credential, or run a transparent donation ledger — all on the same open infrastructure that already runs Agroverse, SunMint, and Edgar.

The pathways

Four ways to join — pick what your program needs

Start with credentials; layer on trees or donations only if they fit. No crypto wallets, no monthly fees — your members never touch any of the machinery.

🎓 Credentialing

Each member gets a public, verifiable credential page (truesight.me/programs/<you>/credentials/) + a printable certificate with a scannable QR. You attest; we issue.

📿 Activity reporting

Members log practice/activity (e.g. a capoeira session); each signed event accrues on their credential as a living record of growth.

🌳 A tree per credential

Associate every attestation with a real cacao tree — a QR-coded tree-planting pledge that links straight back to the member's credential page. We mint the trees automatically.

💸 Donation transparency

Run a public, auditable donation ledger for your program — every inflow, fee, and disbursement visible, with no platform fee on the rails.

What you provide

One definition, then we provision the rest

Your identity

Program details

A short name, a one-paragraph description, your logo, and your website. We create your co-branded program listing + a dedicated admin subdomain (<your-program>.truesight.me) where your team attests members.

Your roster

A cohort sheet

A Google Sheet of your members — shared (Viewer/Editor) with our service account. We mint each member's identity + credential from it; you never re-key anything.

Your choices

Pick your pathways

Tell us which capabilities you want. Trees and donations each get a transparent managed ledger. A DAO governor reviews your request, submits it as a proposal for community voting, and provisions your program after DAO approval.
