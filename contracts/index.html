TrueSight DAO Protocol

Smart Contracts

Every action in the TrueSight DAO is an RSA-signed smart contract. Each contract is a cryptographically signed event submitted to Edgar — the DAO's execution layer — which verifies the signature, applies state transitions, and records the result on the ledger and TrueChain audit trail.

Each payload below is a real example — copy it, sign with your RSA key, and submit to Edgar at edgar.truesight.me to see the contract execute.

SHA-256 Hash Function

🧪 Try It

Paste a signed payload below to verify it against Edgar. Works with any of the 30 contract types listed on this page.

Protocol Architecture

The four-layer architecture of the TrueSight DAO protocol. Every signed event flows from the DApp through the smart contract layer to Edgar, which verifies and records it.

How Signing Works

1

Generate Keypair

Create an RSA-2048 keypair in the DApp or via the CLI. Your public key becomes your DAO identity.

2

Register Identity

Submit an [EMAIL REGISTERED EVENT] to bind your public key to your email. Verify via the link sent to your inbox.

3

Sign a Contract

Choose an event type, fill in the fields, and sign the canonical payload with your private key.

4

Submit to Edgar

POST the signed payload to edgar.truesight.me. Edgar verifies the signature and executes the state transition.

Email Registered Event [EMAIL REGISTERED EVENT]

Register a new email address with the DAO. The first step to creating a DAO identity — binds an email to a public key. This is the entry point for all new members: without a registered and verified email, no other contract can be signed. The DApp generates an RSA-2048 keypair locally, then submits this event to associate the public key with the user's email. Edgar sends a verification link to the provided email to confirm ownership.

Anyone (self-service) Required: Email
Authority: Anyone (self-service)
No authorization required — this is a public registration endpoint. Unauthorized signers are not applicable as this is open to all.
Sheet: Contributors Digital Signatures tab (Main Ledger)

  • Edgar Controller: dao_controller.rb

Email Verification Event [EMAIL VERIFICATION EVENT]

Verify ownership of an email address by signing a challenge. Completes the identity registration flow. After registering an email, the user receives a verification link. Clicking it triggers a signed challenge response that proves the user controls both the email inbox and the private key. Until this step completes, the identity is considered unverified and cannot participate in governance or sign sensitive contracts.

Authority: Anyone with pending registration

Sales Event [SALES EVENT]

This contract is the primary data source for the DAO's income statements.
